Acquisition

Rate Your Acquisition
Grade each question from 1 (not at all) to 5 (absolutely)
01 — How many independent sources regularly bring you new customers?
Think referrals, social media, Google search, paid ads, partnerships, events — each one counts as a separate source. One source means one point of failure. The more you have, the safer your business.
One or none Four or more
02 — Do new leads come in every week without you having to chase them?
Reliable acquisition means your pipeline refills on its own. If leads only appear after you make calls or post something, you don't have a system — you have activity.
Only when I push for it Every week, consistently
03 — If your referrals and personal network dried up tomorrow, would you still get new customers?
Referrals are great, but they're borrowed trust. Businesses that rely almost entirely on word of mouth are one slow season away from a serious problem.
No — referrals are almost everything Yes — other channels are working
04 — Do you run paid ads, or do you rely only on organic reach?
Organic is slow but free. Paid is fast but costs money. The strongest businesses use both. Only organic means slow growth. Only paid means you stop the moment you stop spending.
Only organic or nothing Both paid and organic working together
05 — Do you publish content regularly and does it bring in leads?
Posts, articles, videos, newsletters — content that teaches or helps builds trust at scale. If you publish consistently and people reach out because of it, content is working as a channel for you.
Rarely publish, no leads from it Publish consistently, leads follow
06 — Do you have something free and useful that people can get in exchange for their contact details?
A free guide, a calculator, a case study, a checklist — a lead magnet turns strangers into contacts you can follow up with. Without one, most of your website visitors leave and never come back.
Nothing like this exists Yes, and it brings in contacts regularly
07 — Do you know exactly which channel brings you the most customers, and how much it costs to get one?
Knowing your numbers is what separates a marketing system from marketing activity. If you can't say which channel works best and what a new customer costs you, you can't grow it deliberately.
No idea Know exactly — and optimise it
